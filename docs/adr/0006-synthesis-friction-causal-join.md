# ADR 0006: Join friction evidence to the causal regime

Date: 2026-07-01
Status: accepted

## Context

The causal arm established a transient, corridor-concentrated tourism surge
after the Hokuriku Shinkansen extension. A mechanism layer was needed to
explain the fade and the cap on conversion from improved access to sustained
municipal tourism gains.

## Decision

Join the FTAS municipality and arrival-mode friction feeds to the SCM
per-municipality summary by exact Japanese municipality name. Classify each
municipality as durable, transient, or none while retaining SCM fit as a
separate confidence flag. Combine the resulting cross-municipality pattern and
arrival-mode contrast with the SEM nudge-priority feed in a priority × causal
opportunity matrix.

FTAS is intercept-survey evidence. We use its magnitudes and rank ordering to
identify patterns, not as population-effect estimates.

Feed A, the 17-row SCM per-municipality summary, is a commit-pinned cross-arm
input produced by the national synthetic-control arm, not regenerated by this
repository. It is stored at `data/causal/fukui_municipalities_scm.csv` and
guarded by a SHA-256 checksum. This is intentionally distinct from the other
three synthesis feeds, which are regenerated under `output/` by `build-ftas`
and `nudge-ranking`.

The proper long-term fix is for the causal arm to provide a producer target
that emits this 17-row summary. Until that producer exists, Feed A remains a
committed, frozen, checksum-pinned fixture.

## Consequences

Transport/last-mile access is the single dominant intervention target across
four independent axes: SEM priority, arrival-mode contrast, association with
leaked lift, and causal-opportunity score. `make synthesis` reproducibly emits
the three supporting tables and machine-readable narrative metrics. Figures and
the thesis narrative remain separate thesis artifacts.

## Caveats

- Intercept-survey selection means the 7.09% Shinkansen-arrival prevalence is a
  floor, not a population estimate.
- `市町村` records response location, not respondent residence.
- The cross-municipality analysis has 13 high-confidence observations and is
  pattern-level evidence.
- The cross-arrival-mode contrast is the load-bearing quantitative result.
