# Nudge Priority Ranking (evidence-weighted)

Derived from the two-stage FTAS SEM: a code's priority is the expected
visit-intention damage it transmits (path x prevalence x satisfaction->intention
path of 0.801), i.e. the ceiling a nudge targeting it can recover.
Non-negative satisfaction paths score 0 (no damage to recover).

## Top-3 intervention candidates
### 1. Transport / Access (pre-arrival / en-route)
Publish a consolidated English-language transit guide at Fukui Station and on the DMO website covering bus, train, and rental options to key POIs; note infrequent service and recommend combining destinations on the same route.

### 2. Opening Hours / Availability (pre-visit)
Standardise and keep up-to-date: Google Maps hours, official website, and physical entrance signage; flag seasonal closures at least 4 weeks in advance on all channels; add a 'check before you visit' note on DMO listings for POIs with variable hours.

### 3. Cleanliness / Comfort (on-site)
Introduce a visible scheduled maintenance log at restrooms and high-footfall areas; add seating and shade at key viewing points where practicable.
